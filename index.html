Studying & Working Abroad

Living abroad

The 4 months I spent living in Seville, Spain - a beautiful city in the southern region of Andalusia - were simply amazing. I enjoyed the opportunity to be immersed in the culture and I formed many new friendships. I took advantage of the close proximity to the rest of Europe and travelled often, broadening my horizons further.

It was also a time of both personal and professional growth. Adjusting to a new culture was difficult at times, and learning the language did not come as easily as I had hoped. However, for every difficulty I encountered, I found that I was always rewarded for the hard work I put in to adjust to the cultural differences and the foreign language.

Working abroad

I interned with a small group of Spanish developers and had the opportunity to experience working in another culture. My coworkers spoke varying levels of English, and this forced me to think carefully about what I wanted to say whether because I did not want to hinder their understanding of me with excess, meaningless words, or because my Spanish is limited and I was only able to say things in a straightforward way. This experience has made me speak more intentionally whenever I speak, and has made me a stronger communicator overall.
